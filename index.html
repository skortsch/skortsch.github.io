I am a community and food web ecologist currently working as a University project researcher at Åbo Akademi University, Finland. In my current position, I will investigate the main drivers behind the structure of the Gulf of Riga food web, Baltic Sea. Further, I will be mentoring two PhD students in the field of food web ecology, Lucinda Kraufvelin and Patrik Ståhl.

During my PhD, I was working with Dr. Raul Primicerio (UiT the Arctic University of Norway) where I was introduced to food web ecology. In my PhD I studied variation in food web structure along large-scale environmental gradients. Further I looked at ecological benthic community responses to climate warming. After my PhD I completed postdoctoral work with Dr. Marie Nordström (Åbo Akademi, Finland). This work included linking temporal food web structure in the Baltic Sea (Gulf of Riga) to ecosystem functioning. I recently completed my second postdoctoral research position with Dr. Tomas Roslin at the University of Helsinki, Helsinki, Finland. During my postdoc at HI, I built a individual-based model to predict how the pollination process unfolds in a spatial context.

Outside of research, I like to do outdoor activities depending on the season. I also love saunas and icy swims afterwards.
