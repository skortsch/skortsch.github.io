I am a marine community and food web ecologist currently working as a University researcher at University of Helsinki in Finland. In my current position, I am working on biodiversity changes and carbon cycling in coastal food webs in the Baltic Sea. I am mentoring two PhD students (Lucinda Kraufvelin and Patrik Ståhl) in food web analyses and bioenergtic food web modelling.

During my PhD, I was working with Dr. Raul Primicerio at UiT the Arctic University of Norway, where I was introduced to food web ecology and network analyses. In my PhD, I studied variation in marine food web structure along large-scale environmental gradients in the Barents Sea (sub-Arctic to Arctic region), with special emphasis on how biological responses to climate warming (e.g., poleward shifts) change food web structure and functioning. Further, I studied benthic community responses (regime shifts) to climate warming in Svalbard. After my PhD I completed postdoctoral work with Dr. Marie Nordström (Åbo Akademi, Finland). This work included linking temporal food web structure in the Baltic Sea (Gulf of Riga) to ecosystem functioning using bioenergtic food web modelling. I recently completed my second postdoctoral research position with Dr. Tomas Roslin at the University of Helsinki (HU) in Finland. During my postdoc at HU, I developed a individual-based model to predict how pollination unfolds in a spatial context and how landscape structure and network structure interact to influence pollination.

Outside of research, I like to do outdoor activities depending on the season. I also love going to saunas and taking icy swims afterwards.
