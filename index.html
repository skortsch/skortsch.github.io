Hello, welcome! (New GitHub pages website is under construction!) I am a marine community and food web ecologist at University of Helsinki in Finland @Tvärminne Zoological Station. Broadly, my work focuses on the relationship between biodiversity and ecosystem functioning and stability, and the role that species interactions (trophic and non-trophic) play in maintaining species co-existence and persistence in complex ecological communities. At Tvärminne Zoological Station, I am leading the Food Web Research Group, where we combine empirical and theoretical approaches to food web ecology, ranging from carbon flow modeling, DNA-based methods for food web quantification, to mesocosm experiments and fieldwork. We mainly focus on coastal ecosystems in the Baltic Sea, but I have also previously worked with with food webs from the North Sea, Barents Sea, and Atlantic and Southern Ocean.

In 2024, I was awarded an Academy fellowship by the Research Council of Finland for the project 'Heatwave-driven rewiring of coastal food webs'. The overarching aim of this project is to predict how coastal ecosystems respond to marine heatwaves, a common feature of climate change and one of the most pervasive threats to marine biodiversity globally.

I am also part of the Centre for Coastal Ecosystem and Climate Change Research at University of Helsinki and Stockholm University which evaluates the links between coastal biodiversity, carbon cycling, and climate feedbacks. For more information, please visit the CoastClim website here. In CoastClim, it is my role to assess the importance of biodiversity, and especially species interactions, for carbon cycling and turnover in coastal (Baltic Sea) ecosystems. --> Bit about my background: During my PhD, I was working with Dr. Raul Primicerio at UiT the Arctic University of Norway, where I was introduced to food web ecology and network analyses. In my PhD, I studied variation in marine food web structure along large-scale environmental gradients in the Barents Sea (sub-Arctic to Arctic region), with special emphasis on how biological responses to climate warming (e.g., poleward shifts) change food web structure and functioning. Further, I studied benthic community responses (regime shifts) to climate warming in Svalbard. After my PhD I completed postdoctoral work with Dr. Marie Nordström (University of Helsinki, Finland). This work included linking temporal food web structure in the Baltic Sea (Gulf of Riga) to ecosystem functioning using bioenergtic food web modelling. I completed my second postdoctoral research position with Dr. Tomas Roslin at the University of Helsinki (HU) in Finland. During my postdoc at HU, I developed a individual-based model together with colleagues to predict how pollination unfolds in a spatial context and how landscape structure and network structure interact to influence pollination.

Outside of research, I like to do outdoor activities depending on the season.
