I am a marine community and food web ecologist at University of Helsinki in Finland @Tvärminne Zoological Station. Broadly speaking, my work focuses on the relationship between biodiversity and ecosystem functioning and stability. More specifically, most of my work focuses on the role of species interactions (trophic and non-trophic) in maintaining species co-exsistence and persistence in complex ecological communties. I am mentoring two PhD students (Lucinda Kraufvelin and Patrik Ståhl) in food web analyses and bioenergtic food web modelling.

During my PhD, I was working with Dr. Raul Primicerio at UiT the Arctic University of Norway, where I was introduced to food web ecology and network analyses. In my PhD, I studied variation in marine food web structure along large-scale environmental gradients in the Barents Sea (sub-Arctic to Arctic region), with special emphasis on how biological responses to climate warming (e.g., poleward shifts) change food web structure and functioning. Further, I studied benthic community responses (regime shifts) to climate warming in Svalbard. After my PhD I completed postdoctoral work with Dr. Marie Nordström (Åbo Akademi, Finland). This work included linking temporal food web structure in the Baltic Sea (Gulf of Riga) to ecosystem functioning using bioenergtic food web modelling. I completed my second postdoctoral research position with Dr. Tomas Roslin at the University of Helsinki (HU) in Finland. During my postdoc at HU, I developed a individual-based together with colleagues model to predict how pollination unfolds in a spatial context and how landscape structure and network structure interact to influence pollination.
