General

My work revolves around two main reserach topics:
1. linking ecological network structure to ecosystem functioning, and recently stability
2. studying how ecological networks vary through time and space in response to environmental variability and perturbations from e.g., climate change.

Most of the systems I study are marine, but I have recently ventured out into the fascinating world of plant-pollinator interactions.

Current research projects

Effects of marine heatwaves on food web structure and functioning


Together with Lucinda Kraufevlin, Christian Pansch and Marie Nordstöm at ÅAU, I am performing bioenergetic food web modelling using data from mesocosm communities with data retrieved from previous mesocosm experiments. The analyses will be part of LKs PhD thesis.

Linking ecological network structure to ecosystem functioning


In my recent postdoctoral project at the University of Helsinki (2020-2022), I studied how landscape configurations and network structure interact to infuence plant-pollinator encounter rates with consequences for the pollination process, using an individual-based modeling approach

Food web variability along environmental gradients from the Atlantic to the Antarctic


I am currently involved in an international food web project with an Argententian colleagues Dr. Leonardo Saravia and Dr. Tomas Marina from (Centro Austral de Investigaciones Científicas) that aims to assess the anthropogenic effects and environmental variability in food web structure along a latitudinal gradient from the Atlantic to Southwest-Antarctica?
